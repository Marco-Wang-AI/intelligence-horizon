# v1.1 Survey Plan

The goal is to make the public vote feel like a quick interaction, not homework.

## Required questions

Only four questions should be required:

1. When do you think AGI arrives?
2. When do you think ASI arrives?
3. Which definition is closest to your view of AGI?
4. Which definition is closest to your view of ASI?

Expected completion time: about 45 seconds.

## Optional questions

- Work or study field.
- When AI can do roughly 90% of that field's work.
- A custom one-sentence AGI definition.
- A custom one-sentence ASI definition.
- Display name and public attribution consent.
- Age range.
- A suggested news item, product launch, interview, or expert claim for the Radar Log.

## Participation ideas

Start without cash rewards. Red packets or prize draws can increase volume, but they also attract low-intent responses and make the small early sample less meaningful.

Prefer these lightweight incentives first:

- Say clearly that the core vote takes about 45 seconds.
- Show how the visitor compares with the expert forecast after submitting.
- Feature selected community definitions with optional attribution.
- Publish a biweekly result snapshot so participants have a reason to return.
- Invite one sourced signal for the next Radar Log.

If response volume remains too low, test a small, clearly disclosed prize draw later and keep those responses marked as an incentivized sample.
