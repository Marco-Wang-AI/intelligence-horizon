# v1.1.0 - The radar becomes a radar

This release makes the central metaphor more consistent: signals now move closer to or farther from the center, measured in playful relative radar meters.

## Highlights

- **Radar first on mobile.** The AGI radar now appears immediately instead of waiting below a large cover image.
- **Mobile information, reorganized.** The biweekly update sits inside the AGI card, while expert and public forecasts remain side by side.
- **Swipe between views.** AGI / ASI radars and definition cards now form horizontal mobile tracks with a visible next-card preview.
- **Faces replace initials.** Radar signals use the project's illustrated expert and product avatars.
- **The sweep selects signals.** As the radar scans each avatar, the related event and distance appear automatically.
- **Meters replace days.** Events show how much closer or farther they move the radar.
- **Clearer for newcomers.** AGI and ASI are expanded on the cards, and the large year is explained as the current expert forecast.
- **Signals, recalibrated.** Distances now reflect evidence strength more consistently; OpenClaw is credited to Peter Steinberger and dated to its January 2026 rename.
- **Faster participation.** Each radar card now includes a compact shortcut to the survey.
- **Sources are one click away.** Radar Log entries and selected events link directly to their public source.
- **A shorter Radar Log.** Mobile readers can swipe between AGI and ASI signal columns, and the internal Method Desk placeholder is gone.
- **Definitions can move too.** Leading definition candidates are reviewed on the same biweekly rhythm as the radar.
- **A shorter public survey.** Four core choices are required; everything else is optional.
- **A friendlier open invitation.** The project now points people directly to GitHub and the signal proposal form.
- **Final mobile polish.** Left-side distance labels stay inside the radar, and Radar Log headings no longer repeat AGI / ASI.
